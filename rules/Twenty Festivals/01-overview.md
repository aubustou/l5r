# 01 — Game Overview, Cardinal Rules, Changes From Ivory
 
## Game Overview
 
**L5R: Twenty Festivals** is a two-player (multiplayer-capable) collectible card game set in the fictional Empire of Rokugan. Each player represents a daimyō (clan leader) commanding Personalities, Holdings, Strategies and magic to pursue one of several win conditions.
 
### Components each player brings
 
- **1 Stronghold** (two-sided; face shown depends on going-first/going-second).
- **Optionally 1 Sensei** (starts in play if included).
- **Dynasty deck** (black-backed): Personalities, Holdings, Events, Regions.
- **Fate deck** (green-backed): Strategies, Followers, Items, Spells, Rings.
- **4 Provinces**, arrayed between the Dynasty deck and Fate deck.
- **Hand** of up to 8 Fate cards (start of game: 5).
- **Optional tokens**, physical markers, and a way to track Family Honor.
### Communal / shared
 
- **Imperial Favor** — a single token contested by both players via the Lobby ability.
- **Battlefields** — created during attacks; communal.
- **"Outside the game"** pile — removed-from-game cards.
### Win conditions (detail in `02-players-victory-decks.md`)
 
1. **Military Victory** — opponent has no Provinces remaining.
2. **Honor Victory** — start your turn on 40 Family Honor or higher.
3. **Dishonor Loss / Victory** — a player at –20 or lower Family Honor at the end of his or her turn loses; the last remaining opponent wins.
4. **Enlightenment Victory** — control 5 Rings with five different element keywords (Air, Earth, Fire, Water, Void).
5. **Special Victory Conditions** — as defined on individual cards.

## Cardinal Rules
 
The three Cardinal Rules establish the rule-resolution hierarchy.
 
### CARDINAL RULE 1 — Cards vs. rules
 
> If the text of any card contradicts these Comprehensive Rules, follow the card text, not the rules.
 
Something that changes one aspect of a rule does not by itself change any other aspects of the rule.
 
**Example (source):** An effect that lets you make a Ranged Attack against a card in a player's home only changes the rule that the target of a Ranged Attack must be in the current enemy army; it does not change the rule that the target of a Ranged Attack must be a Follower or a Personality without Followers.
 
### CARDINAL RULE 2 — Cards vs. cards (MRP)
 
> New versions of cards with a given identity (title and experience level) may sometimes be printed. If different printings contradict each other, follow the most recent Ivory-legal printing of the card, or the most recent printing if the card was never Ivory-legal.
 
(This is the "Most Recent Printing" rule.)
 
### CARDINAL RULE 3 — Rules vs. rules
 
> If the text of any other rules document (such as the rulebook or rulesheet in starter boxes) contradicts these Comprehensive Rules, follow the most recent online version of the Comprehensive Rules.
 
### The Good Faith Rule
 
A player may only announce an action if it will have at least one effect, taking into account known future game-state changes (e.g. costs that will bow cards, destruction of cards, etc.). See `06-gold-actions-timing.md` §Good Faith.

## Changes From Ivory Edition
 
These are the seven changes introduced by the Twenty Festivals rules update, quoted from the Comprehensive Rules header:
 
1. **Gold production is now an official stat on Holdings**, and they can bow to produce that amount of Gold as part of the rules.
2. **A new wording specifies when Gold production can only be used for a single purpose.**
3. **Equip and Recruit now target the card they are bringing into play.**
4. **In the action sequence there is a "choose targets" step after costs are paid and before Interrupts are taken**, although actual targeting that triggers effects is still taken in action resolution, normally based on the earlier choices.
5. **Fortifications enter play bowed by default.**
6. **Shugenja is no longer a boldface keyword**; the rules that Spells can only attach to them are now more clearly a rule about Spells, not Shugenja.
7. **Addition of the Unstoppable and Legacy keywords.**
