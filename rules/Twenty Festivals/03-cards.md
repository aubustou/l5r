# 03 — Card Types
 
All L5R cards fall into one of several types, each with its own frame, stats, and rules.
 
## Starting Card Types
 
### Stronghold
 
A two-sided card that does **not** go into the Dynasty or Fate deck and is **neither a Dynasty nor Fate card**. A player starts with it in play; the choice of Stronghold is part of deck construction.
 
- **Going-first side face up** (one clan mon, black border) if the player is going first; otherwise the reverse side (two clan mons, white border) is used.
- **Clan Mon**: next to the title, indicates Clan Alignment (no mon = Unaligned).
- **Province Strength** stat: topmost of three right-side icons — starting strength of each of the player's Provinces.
- **Gold Production (GP)**: middle coin icon — Gold produced when bowed. A stat affected by tokens and effects.
- **Starting Family Honor**: bottom icon — sets the player's starting Family Honor.
- **Stronghold Immunity**: effects and costs will **not** make a Stronghold leave play unless they specifically mention Strongholds.
- An object incorporating a legal Stronghold card can be used as long as it shows all stats, text, and symbols of the original.
### Sensei
 
A starting card with **modifiers** for Province Strength, Gold Production, and Starting Family Honor which are **continually applied** to the Stronghold's stats. A Sensei may also have traits and abilities.
 
- Not a Dynasty or Fate card; does not go in either deck.
- A play deck may have **only one Sensei**; it starts in play if included.
- **Modifiers from a Sensei are treated like any other modifier** — they do not modify the printed values on the Stronghold.
- Sensei traits and abilities are **not** on the Stronghold; Sensei cards bow and have abilities separately.
- Clan restrictions are **not keywords** despite appearing in the keyword area.
## Dynasty Card Types
 
### Holding
 
Holdings have **metallic bronze frames** and represent a player's resources. Often bow to produce Gold.
 
- Have a **Gold Cost** (central round yellow coin icon).
- Use the **Recruit** action or procedure to enter play.
- **Enter play bowed** (already bowed without bowing).
- **Fortification** is a keyword on Holdings with special rules (see `11-keyword-reference.md`).
#### Holding Gold Production (GP)
 
An optional stat in the yellow coin icon at top right. Amount of Gold produced when the Holding is bowed for Gold production.
 
- **Gold-producing Holdings** have this stat; non-Gold-producing Holdings do not have the icon or the stat, so their Gold Production **cannot be raised**.
- GP can be changed by card effects and tokens.
### Personality
 
Personalities represent the samurai, courtiers, and other figures serving a clan. They are central units in battle.
 
- **Clan Mon**: title-bar mon indicates Clan Alignment (or none = Unaligned).
- Earlier Ivory-legal cards indicate Clan Alignment with both mon and keyword; later cards indicate it only with the mon but are **considered to have the keyword**.
- Enter play using the **Recruit** action or procedure.
- A Personality and attached cards make up a **unit**.
#### Personality stats
 
- **Force (F)** — lighter colored icon at top left.
- **Chi (C)** — dark colored icon at top right.
- **Honor Requirement (HR)** — leftmost of three middle icons (square banner). A dash (–) means infinitely low HR — always lower than any numeric HR. HR may be lower than zero.
- **Gold Cost** — round yellow coin — paid to Recruit.
- **Personal Honor (PH)** — the fan icon (rightmost of the middle three).
#### Honor Requirement behavior
 
- A player **cannot bring a Personality into play with HR higher than his or her current Family Honor**.
- If anything but a player's **own** cards causes him or her to lose Honor (other players' cards or rulebook losses such as dying dishonorably), then for the **rest of the game** that player **ignores Honor Requirements** when bringing Personalities with his or her Clan Alignment into play.
#### Recruiting Clan-aligned Personalities (Clan Honor Rule)
 
The player pays an **additional 2 Gold** to Recruit a Personality that has a Clan Alignment but does not have the player's Clan Alignment. (See Recruit action in `08-turn-structure.md` §Dynasty Phase.)
 
#### Chi Death Rule
 
> If a Personality's Chi is ever zero, destroy him immediately. Effects that prevent a single instance of destruction will not prevent this; only continuous effects work against Chi death (for example, "This Personality will not be destroyed.").
 
### Event
 
Events have **white and gray scroll frames** and represent important happenings in Rokugan.
 
- While face-up in their player's Provinces, **their abilities can be used**.
- If they enter play, their **traits are active** while in play, but their **abilities cannot be used unless otherwise stated**.
- Events in Provinces are **discarded once an ability on them is used**, unless the ability puts the Event itself into play.
### Region
 
Regions are a Dynasty card type included in the Twenty Festivals edition and listed in the Advanced Rulebook's card-type reference. They follow the general card-type rules for Dynasty cards; specific region mechanics appear on individual cards. Regions occupy Provinces and obey Dynasty-area rules.
 
## Fate Card Types
 
All Fate cards have a **Focus Value** stat (black circle at the bottom of the card) — used in duels and some effects.
 
### Strategy
 
Strategy cards are **normally played from the hand** to use an ability on them.
 
- When played from any area, they go to an **entering-play / resolution area** while Good Faith is checked, costs are paid, and effects resolve.
- Once the action has finished resolving, the player **discards the Strategy**, unless the Strategy put itself into play.
- A Strategy usually has one or more abilities. Traits on a Strategy always modify its own abilities, and may affect play more generally **if** the Strategy enters play.
- If a Strategy enters play, its abilities may be used as normal.
- **Terrain** is a Strategy keyword — see `11-keyword-reference.md`.
### Ring
 
A player may put a Ring into play from his or her hand **immediately after fulfilling its condition for entering play**. This may not be delayed. A condition may be fulfilled **more than once** by new occurrences.
 
**Example (source):** If a player takes a third Favor action after having already taken two, he or she can play the Ring of Air after the third.
 
A Ring's text may also let a player discard it from his or her hand for an effect. Normally this effect uses the Ring's ability without cost; the Ring enters a **resolution area** while the relevant ability is resolving and is **discarded afterwards**.
 
### Attachments — Followers, Items, Spells
 
Followers, Items, and Spells are all **attachment cards**. They normally attach to Personalities in play using the **Equip** procedure, placed under the Personality with the title showing. Attaching requires choosing or targeting a Personality.
 
- Attachments may **only enter play** by being attached to a Personality.
- Attachments are **discarded if in play and not attached to a Personality**.
- **Only attachments may attach to a Personality.**
- A player may only attach attachments to a Personality he or she **controls**.
- **Gold Cost**: attachments have a Gold Cost stat paid when Equipping them.
#### Follower
 
- Yellow-brown background.
- **Force** (top left) helps their unit in battle.
- A Follower's Force is **independent** and does not contribute in battle resolution if the **Follower** is bowed (regardless of whether the Personality is bowed).
#### Item
 
- Dark blue background with sword frame at bottom of artwork.
- **Force modifier** (top left icon): directly raises/lowers the Personality's Force.
- **Chi modifier**: direct raise/lower of Personality's Chi.
- **Item modifiers are NOT bonuses or penalties**, but affect stats the same way — including being subject to maximum and minimum values.
- If something in the game needs to check an Item's Force or Chi separately from its Personality's stats, use the **value of its modifier (minimum zero)**.
- **Force and Chi bonuses or penalties to an Item affect the modifier**; e.g., a +1F Item that suffers –2F now has a –1F modifier.
- A Personality can only have **one Weapon** attached. EXCEPTION: Kensai (see `11-keyword-reference.md`).
- A Personality — even a Kensai — cannot attach a **Two-Handed Weapon** if they have a Weapon attached.
#### Spell
 
- Light blue background.
- **No Force or Chi stats or modifiers.**
- **Only attach to a Shugenja Personality**; abilities can only be used ("cast") if attached to a Shugenja.
- The "Spells attach only to Shugenja" rule is a rule about Spells, not Shugenja (per the 20F change).
## Card Features
 
### Card Title
 
At the top of every card is its title bar. All L5R cards have a title, **except created cards**, which have no title.
 
The text at the top of proxy created cards printed by AEG is for reference only and does **not constitute a title**.
 
References to "different" and "same" titles follow the rules for "different" and "same" keywords (see `04-stats-keywords-abilities.md`).
 
### Card Subtitles
 
Subtitles in smaller font inside the title bar — and the comma separating them from the card title — are **not part of the card title**.
 
**Example (source):** *Hiruma Nikaru, The Flesh Eater* has the title "Hiruma Nikaru" for play purposes.
 
Subtitles can be referred to by game text; e.g., *Mirumoto Tsuda* with the subtitle "Emerald Champion" counts as an "Emerald Champion" card.
 
### Text Box
 
Every card has a text box that can contain up to three game-relevant elements:
 
- **Keywords**: printed in all caps above a separating line.
- **Traits**: normal text in the text box.
- **Abilities**: normal text preceded by boldface designators (**Battle:**, **Limited:**, **Open:**, **Engage:**, **Dynasty:**, **Interrupt:**).
The text box also includes color text and flavor text (no game impact).
 
### Flavor, color, credit, reminder text
 
- **Flavor text**: italic at the bottom of the text box; no game impact.
- **Color text**: italic, no parentheses, describes what effects are simulating; no game impact. Always a separate sentence.
- **Credit text** (expansion/number/rarity/artist along the lower edge; story credits along the right edge): no game impact.
- **Reminder text**: italic in parentheses — helps players remember wording implications. Carries no rules implications itself; may describe effects in a shortened or non-standard way (e.g., using "dies," which does not exist in game rules, for "is destroyed").
### Created Cards
 
Some effects may create cards in addition to those included in decks. Players may use pieces of paper, face-down spare cards, or other objects to track them.
 
- If an effect that creates a card gives no value for a stat the card type normally has, that stat's base value is **zero**.
- **EXCEPTION**: Created Personalities have a "–" Honor Requirement.
- Created attachments attach to a Personality as part of their creation effect.
- Other created cards enter play in your home; cards created during a battle join your current army, attaching to a Personality if necessary.
- When a created card leaves play, it **ceases to exist**.
